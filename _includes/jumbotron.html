Who Governs the Governor’s Council?

The Governor’s Council is an eight-member elected body that, among other functions, vets all judges and members of the Parole Board and votes on pardons and commutations. Despite its importance, few voters know its functions nor their own Governor’s Councilors.

One of the least well-known elected positions in Massachusetts is that of Governor’s Councilor. The purpose of this project is to educate voters so they will be more aware of its importance, including its role in the criminal legal system, and more informed when they vote.
