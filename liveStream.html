Records of Council meetings

Public Records in Massachusetts are available according to Chapter 66, Section 10 of the Massachusetts General Laws. Records of agencies and municipalities are presumed to be public. However when Laura Berland (see youtube video ) asked the Governor’s Council executive secretary for voting information by the Council, she was told that it was so time intensive that she would have to go into the office and get it herself.

This 30 second video, made by Jean Trounstine on January 30th at the State House shows Laura with a fraction of the stacks of paper documents she would have to go through to find out the voting records of each counselor on nominees. In this case, Laura spent more than two hours going through paper document records. No votes are digitized or recorded any other way except by hand.

Voting History

In 2022, the Council voted 102 times on nominees for judges (49), clerk magistrates (17), Industrial Accident Board(12), Parole Board (5), Appellate Tax Board (3), public administrators (2), pardons (11) and commutations (3).

*Why is this voting pattern a problem?*

  • Out of 102 votes:
  • DePalo: 13 No votes, 2 voted “present” (abstained)
  • Duff: 6 Nos 1 abstain
  • Devaney: 5 Nos 1 abstain
  • Hurley: 3 Nos
  • Kennedy: 1 No
  • Ferreira: 0 Nos
  • Iannella: 0 Nos
  • Jubinville: 0 Nos, 2 recusals (one for himself and one for his daughter)
